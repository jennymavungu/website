Our aim is to promote the well-being of people physically, emotionally and spiritually and to raise other problems young children, youth and elderly people are facing today through our organisation

Our purpose is to find a solution for vulnerable people who are abused sexually, physically, mentally and those who suffer with mental health condition. Help the youth who are involved in crime, rape, abuse, etc to understand they can do something better in the society with all their potentials

Our vision is to change and transform today society by improving people's lives in Greater Manchester, improve their well-being, give them a chance for a better life

Our mission is to give opportunity to people of all ages to Support themselves through providing practical support, friendship, connections into work, improvements to health and well-being and new experiences to achieve their goals

Our organisation value: Faith House has developed a set of ethics that support all we do and how we work:

Belief in people: Inspiring children, youth, the elderly and the community to achieve their goals Empowering others: we want to see the people we work with, including clients, volunteers, staff and supporters, equipped and released to discover meaning and purpose in their lives. Showing compassion, love and respect where everyone is comfortable. Diversity: Acceptance the differences and valuing the contribution of all.

Prospect: Growth and improvement is at the centre of everything we do Partnership: Working collectively to build strong relationships based on mutual trust

Committed to excellence: we strive for professional excellence with integrity in all our working practices.

Restless for justice: We adopt a non-judgmental approach and treat everyone equally valuable, regardless of race, religion, beliefs, sexuality, gender or social standing.

Servant-hearted: we adopt an attitude of service within the organisation and beyond, putting the needs of others before our own. Dignity for all /Generous: welcome every person we meet, sharing hospitality, kindness and love, and treating everyone with dignity and respect, regardless of their background. By sustaining and implanting these values, we define how we collaborate the Faith House way.

Faith House was founded in 2019 to address the needs of the growing numbers of vulnerable people in the UK and in Africa, whose their life have been ruined, marginalised or neglected and who had nowhere to turn to for help. Faith House was named by its founders, Jenny Mavungu and partnerships, who were inspired by one of the parables in the bible. It is found in Hebrews chapter 11, verse1. “Now faith is confidence in what we hope for and assurance about what we do not see”. “Faith can move mountains. If someone beliefs and confidence are strong enough, they can achieve something that is very difficult”. The Faith House works to do the same putting our faith into action to improve people's life in UK and Africa no matter how the society works.
